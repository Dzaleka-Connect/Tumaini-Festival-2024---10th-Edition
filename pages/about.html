About Tumaini Festival

Africa's Premier Refugee-Led Arts & Cultural Festival

Mission

Tumaini Festival, founded in 2014 by Menes La Plume, has evolved into Malawi's largest multicultural event. The festival brings together refugees and locals through artistic expression, promoting intercultural harmony, mutual understanding, and peaceful coexistence between refugees and host communities.

Vision

The festival envisions a world where cultural diversity is celebrated, refugees are empowered through arts and culture, and communities unite to create lasting positive change. Through its annual gathering, Tumaini Festival continues to break down barriers and foster understanding between different cultures.

Core Values

Unity

The festival connects diverse communities through shared artistic experiences, fostering understanding and collaboration.

Empowerment

Through the arts, the festival creates platforms for refugees to showcase their talents and build sustainable livelihoods.

Diversity

The festival celebrates the rich tapestry of cultures, traditions, and artistic expressions from around the world.

History

What began as a small cultural gathering in Dzaleka Refugee Camp has grown into a major international arts festival. Throughout the years, Tumaini Festival has hosted hundreds of artists from across Africa and beyond, establishing itself as a unique platform for cultural exchange and artistic expression. The festival's growth reflects its commitment to transforming lives through the power of arts and culture.
